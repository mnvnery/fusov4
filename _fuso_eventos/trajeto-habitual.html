It delves into the unpredictability of our existence, highlighting what Alberto Caeiro referred to as "the astonishing reality of things" in contrast to their impermanence and ephemerality. The performance portrays a body in constant motion, reminiscent of a carefree child exploring its boundaries, all within the same fluid movement. It evokes the feeling of dizziness and the resulting shift in perception while enduring as a method for comprehending reality and conveying emotional expression.
